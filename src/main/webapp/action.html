Why does this matter?

Conservationists say that we are in the middle of the sixth mass extinction due to human activity in the last couple centuries. Wildlife reports have concluded the following things:

  • The rapid loss of species we are seeing today is estimated by experts to be between 1,000 and 10,000 times higher than the natural extinction rate.
  • Populations of mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, and amphibians have, on average, declined 60% between 1970 and 2014. 1
  • Up to 1 million of the estimated 8 million plant and animal species on Earth are at risk of extinction — many of them within decades. 2
  • Global biodiversity is being lost much faster than natural extinction due to changes in land use, unsustainable use of natural resources, invasive alien species, climate change and pollution among others. 3

Millions of species have already gone extinct and many more will continue to do so unless we make big steps towards sustainability. If this trend continues, our ecosystems’ ability to sustain the planet’s species and meet human needs will be threatened.


Where can I learn more about conservation?

The rate at which animal species are becoming endangered and extinct is increasing at an exponential rate. Here are some organizations you can follow that work towards protecting endangered animals.

The World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

The World Wildlife Fund strives to ensure the world’s most iconic species, including tigers, rhinos, and elephants, are secured and recovering in the wild. For nearly 60 years, they have made it their mission to find solutions that save the marvelous array of life on our planet by applying the best science available and working closely with local communities.

Project AWARE Foundation

Project AWARE is a global movement for ocean protection powered by a community of adventurers. They connect the passion for ocean adventure with the purpose of marine conservation. For over two decades, they have been supporting and operating ocean conservation projects through scuba dive-based marine conservation in our world’s oceans.

International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)

IUCN is a membership Union composed of both government and civil society organisations. It harnesses the experience, resources and reach of its more than 1,400 Member organisations and the input of more than 15,000 experts. This diversity and vast expertise makes IUCN the global authority on the status of the natural world and the measures needed to safeguard it.

The Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS)

WCS was founded in 1895 and aims to combine science, conservation and education to discover and understand the natural world. This knowledge helps them engage and inspire decision-makers, communities, and millions of supporters to take action with WCS to protect the wildlife and wild places of our planet.
